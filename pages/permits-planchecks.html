Permit and Plancheck Items

Go to Interactive Checklist

Permits and planchecks are critical elements in the construction process, ensuring that your project meets all local, state, and federal requirements before, during, and after construction.

This category includes architectural approvals, building permits, specialized permits for different construction phases, and various agency planchecks that need to be obtained throughout your project lifecycle.

Common Permit Types
  • Architectural Planning Board Approval - Initial approval of architectural plans by the local planning board
  • Building Department Permit - Overall construction permit from the building department
  • Demolition Permit - Required before demolishing existing structures
  • Grading Permit - Required for site preparation and earth moving
  • Foundation Permit - Specifically for foundation work
  • Structural Frame Permit - For structural framing work
  • Electrical/Mechanical Permits - Specific to electrical and mechanical installations
Key Considerations
Timing

Some permits must be obtained sequentially, while others can be processed in parallel. Understanding the correct sequence is crucial for project scheduling.

Costs

Permit fees can vary significantly based on project size, location, and complexity. Budget for these costs early in the project planning.

Responsibility

Clearly define which team members or organizations are responsible for obtaining each permit to avoid delays and ensure accountability.

Compliance

Failure to obtain required permits can result in fines, construction delays, or even legal action. Thoroughly review all requirements before starting construction.
